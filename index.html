Trump-Doonbeg Timeline

The battle for the protected Co. Clare sand dunes and shoreline has been ongoing for 20 years, long before Trump got involved. Let's take a look at the key events.

National Parks Opposes Golf Course

The National Parks and Wildlife Service (NPWS) "strongly opposes" golf course development on intact areas of the dune system, as it would "destroy the ecological..value of the site." They recommend the course is developed in areas where the dunes are already degreaded.

Read more October 1995

Kiawah Partners Purchase Land

US-based Kiawah Partners, developers of other golf courses, purchases the land from Shannon Development, a government arm. Development begins the same month.

Read more December 1999

High Court Action Protects Snail

High Court deems the 2mm Vertigo Angustior snail to be threatened in one of its last habitats, and rules it protected. Greg Norman, the golf course's designed, forced to redesign plans and move course inland.

Read more 2000

Right of Way Access

Local dispute resolved as the club agrees to build a new road to the beach and a car park at a cost of €500,000.

Read more June 2001

Club Begins High Court Proceedings

Local farmers continue to drive tractors across 5th fairway, claiming to have evidence for the right of way dating to 1917. Club maintains that tractors are damaging the course and the protected snail, the Vertigo Angustior is being damaged.

Read more January 2003

Golf Club and Hotel Open

The resort opens, at a cost of €28m. Memberships is set at $20,000. Shannon development expects golfers to spend €14m per year in the area. Landmark National and Kiawah Partners apply for a new clubhouse and 5 star hotel. The Palmer Cup is hosted at Doonbeg, attracting celebrities the world over.

Read more July 2002

Club Constructs Wall Without Planning Permission

The 8ft high wall was constructed in 2004 to stop access to Doughmore beach by vehicle. In 2009, Clare County Council voted to extinguish the right-of-way leading to the wall, and provided an alternative right of way closeby. The Club sought retentino of the wall, but Clare County Council ordered it to be demolished by July 2011 (decision made in 2010?).

Read more 2004

High Court Settlement

2004 - Club settles with 5 locals after a 4 year dispute over the right of way. The club then extinguished the right of way by building the wall, without permission. Mr McNulty said the High Court decision was flawed. Mr McNamara, who onws 4 acres by the cours, appealed unsuccessfully 5 times against the development of the course.

Read more 2004

Coastal Protection Plan Appealed

An Taisce opposed plans for coastal armour on an area designated as a "Special Area of Conservation." It is an appellant to An Bord Pleanala against a decision by Clare County Council to allow it.

Read more 2005

Family Feud Causes Sale of Kiawah Partners

Group and their partners sell Doonbeg, after investing €67m into it. New owners, South Street Partners, move to sell Doonbeg after discovering it was never profitable. Loss of €6.4m in 2011.

Largest Ever Storms Hit

Parts of the sand dunes and course damaged. The back tee – one of four – on the 18th hole at Doonbeg was swept away. A segment of the fairway was also lost there to the elements.

Attempt to build illegal sea wall stopped by council order.

An Board Pleanala Rules Rock Armour Not of Strategic Performance

In the latest twist in the saga, TIGL’s application to have the wall declared a strategic infrastructure development has been denied by An Bord Pleanála. If the application does receive the green light, work is scheduled to start next spring.
